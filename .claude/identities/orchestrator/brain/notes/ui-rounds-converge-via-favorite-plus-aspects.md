When delegating a UI decision to the human, each round should offer 2-4 GENUINELY different options, and the next round should be synthesized from the favorite plus every liked aspect chip — with 1-2 alternatives probing what the picks left ambiguous. This converged a visualizer design in three rounds. Element-level annotations ("this button: too small, move it") are the highest-signal channel; treat each as a scoped instruction, never a general theme. Related: [[gate-recording-lesson]].
